Graph Algorithms Overview

Graph algorithms are fundamental techniques for analyzing and manipulating graph structures, which consist of vertices (nodes) connected by edges. These algorithms are crucial for solving complex problems in:

  • Network routing and optimization
  • Social network analysis
  • Geographic mapping and navigation
  • Resource allocation and scheduling

Types of Graphs

  • Directed vs Undirected: Edges may have direction (one-way) or be bidirectional
  • Weighted vs Unweighted: Edges may have associated costs or distances
  • Cyclic vs Acyclic: Graphs may or may not contain cycles
  • Connected vs Disconnected: All vertices may or may not be reachable

Learning Objectives

  • Master graph representation techniques (adjacency matrix/list)
  • Understand traversal algorithms (DFS, BFS)
  • Learn shortest path algorithms (Dijkstra's, Bellman-Ford)
  • Study minimum spanning tree algorithms (Kruskal's, Prim's)

Real-world Applications

  • GPS and navigation systems
  • Social media friend recommendations
  • Network routing protocols
  • Supply chain optimization
